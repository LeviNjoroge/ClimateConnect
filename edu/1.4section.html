Effects on the Environment

Climate change is having profound and far-reaching effects on the environment, disrupting ecosystems and natural processes that sustain life on Earth. Here are some of the most significant environmental impacts:

Rising Temperatures

  • Global Warming: The Earth's average surface temperature has increased by approximately 1.1°C (2°F) since the late 19th century, with the most rapid warming occurring in recent decades.
  • Heatwaves: More frequent and intense heatwaves are occurring worldwide, stressing ecosystems, wildlife, and human health.
  • Ocean Warming: Oceans absorb over 90% of the excess heat trapped by greenhouse gases, leading to higher sea temperatures and disrupting marine ecosystems.

Extreme Weather Events

  • Intensified Storms: Hurricanes, cyclones, and typhoons are becoming more powerful due to warmer ocean waters, leading to greater destruction.
  • Droughts and Floods: Changes in precipitation patterns are causing prolonged droughts in some regions and severe flooding in others.
  • Wildfires: Hotter, drier conditions are increasing the frequency and intensity of wildfires, destroying forests, homes, and wildlife habitats.

Melting Glaciers and Ice Sheets

  • Polar Ice Loss: The Arctic is warming at twice the global average rate, leading to rapid melting of sea ice and glaciers.
  • Rising Sea Levels: Melting ice sheets in Greenland and Antarctica, along with thermal expansion of seawater, are causing sea levels to rise, threatening coastal communities and ecosystems.
  • Loss of Freshwater Sources: Glaciers are critical sources of freshwater for millions of people. Their melting jeopardizes water supplies for drinking, agriculture, and hydropower.

Biodiversity Loss

  • Habitat Destruction: Rising temperatures, deforestation, and extreme weather are destroying habitats, pushing many species toward extinction.
  • Shifting Ecosystems: Species are migrating to cooler areas, disrupting ecosystems and food chains.
  • Coral Bleaching: Warmer oceans are causing coral reefs to bleach and die, affecting marine biodiversity and the livelihoods of people who depend on them.
