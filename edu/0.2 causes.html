Causes of Climate Change

Natural Causes

  • Solar Variability: Changes in the Sun's energy output can influence Earth's climate.
  • Volcanic Activity: Volcanic eruptions release aerosols and gases that can temporarily cool the planet by reflecting sunlight.
  • Natural Greenhouse Gases: Gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane (CH₄) occur naturally and help regulate Earth's temperature.

Human-Induced Causes

  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions: The burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) releases large amounts of CO₂ and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, trapping heat and leading to global warming.
  • Deforestation: Cutting down forests reduces the planet's capacity to absorb CO₂, exacerbating the greenhouse effect.
  • Agriculture: Livestock farming produces methane, while the use of synthetic fertilizers releases nitrous oxide, both potent greenhouse gases.
  • Industrial Processes: Manufacturing, mining, and chemical production contribute to emissions and pollution.
  • Urbanization: The expansion of cities increases energy consumption and alters land use, further contributing to climate change.
